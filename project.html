Turning art into measurable care.

Across music, theater, dance, visual arts, and writing, we design and study evidence-based programs — delivered in the community and online — for people facing conditions from Parkinson's and dementia to depression, trauma, and isolation.

Built on research. Proven by measurement.

Every program starts from existing science, is fast-tracked into community-based and virtual settings, and is measured so we can show — not just claim — that doses of art improve brain, body, and mental health.

We build a community of teaching artists to deliver these programs to the populations who need them most — lowering health care costs, easing the burden on caregivers, and giving every demographic the chance to heal and grow through the arts.

Iambic pentameter walks at the pace of a footstep.

Our pilot program puts Shakespeare's verse to work for people living with Parkinson's: da-DUM, da-DUM, da-DUM — five beats per line, close to a natural stride. Rhythmic cueing is already used to help steady gait and speech; we extend that idea into full scenes, sonnets, and soliloquies, performed aloud and on your feet.

Participants don't just listen to a beat — they speak it, breathe it, and move to it, in a group setting that prioritizes expression over performance pressure.
